3 Techies Banter Goes Live : We're Launching Our Internet Radio Station!

We are buzzing with excitement to announce that 3 Techies Banter is launching its very own internet radio station! After countless episodes dissecting the latest in tech, we're taking a delightful step sideways—or perhaps backwards—into one of humanity's most enduring technologies: radio.

Why Radio Might Outlive Us All

Here is a thought experiment we love: When aliens arrive or the mushroom clouds bloom, what communication technology will still be standing? The answer - Radio. While our smartphones become expensive paperweights and satellite networks go dark, someone, somewhere, will be cranking up a hand-powered radio transmitter, sending Morse code or voice into the electromagnetic void. Radio waves require only a basic transmitter and receiver for their transmission. They don't require login credentials, software updates, or a stable internet connection. They just... work.

Radio is the cockroach of communication technologies—practically indestructible and frustratingly simple. And we mean that as the highest compliment.

The Magic of the Airwaves

There is something thrilling about radio that podcasts alone cannot quite capture. It is the knowledge that you are tuning into something in that moment, alongside countless invisible fellow listeners scattered across the globe. It is the serendipity of stumbling upon an unexpected song or conversation. It is the gentle static between stations, the distant voices breaking through from faraway places, the feeling of connection without algorithms deciding what you should hear next.

Radio listening is an act of trust and discovery. You surrender control to the DJ, the host, the programming director—and sometimes, you find gold. There's no scrubbing through timestamps, no skipping ahead.

Low in Tech; High on Reliability

We have featured some really cool tech in our podcast, including DPIs at SCALE, quantum cryptography, and other exciting innovations in funtech. But let us be honest: sometimes the old tech is dependable tech too. Like feature phones that last two weeks on a single charge. Ethernet cables that deliver consistent gigabit speeds while your Wi-Fi mysteriously drops for the third time today. Mechanical keyboards that will outlive your grandchildren. AM/FM radios that operate during power outages.

There's profound beauty in technology that refuses to break. No mandatory updates that brick your device. No subscription services that lock you out. No "smart" features that stop working when the company's servers go down. Just pure, reliable functionality.

Our Frequency, Your Adventure

As the 3 Techies Banter team, we couldn't be more thrilled to add "radio broadcasters" to our resumes. We're bringing our signature blend of tech talk, terrible puns, and tangential discussions to the airwaves—both internet and, who knows, maybe someday actual radio frequencies.

Radio isn't just about nostalgia (though we'll admit to a healthy dose of that). It is about creating a unique listening experience.

We're building a station that celebrates both the cutting edge and the tried-and-true, where we can debate the merits of the latest JavaScript framework while broadcasting on technology that predates the transistor. So tune in, turn up, and join us on this wonderfully anachronistic adventure. Because in a world of constant disruption, sometimes the most radical thing you can do is embrace the technology that refuses to be disrupted.

The 3 Techies Banter Radio Station:

Coming to an internet connection near you. And when the internet fails? Well, we'll figure out how to broadcast the old-fashioned way. After all, if radio can survive nuclear winter, it can certainly survive our hosting.
